THE ITHACA APPLE HARVEST FESTIVAL EVENTS

From our four annual festivals to monthly art gallery nights, to free live music & public performances, there is always something going on Downtown!

Gallery Night Ithaca - Every First Friday of the month from 5:00-8:00 PM on the first Friday of every month.

Gallery Night Ithaca presented by Tompkins Trust Company is a walkable tour of art openings and other special cultural events in and around downtown Ithaca, With upwards of twenty venues to visit to explore each month, there is sure to be something that will catch your eye.

Haunted History Tours- October 15 @ 6:00 pm - October 28 @ 9:00 pm.

Although Ithaca might be better known for its gorges, vibrant community, and colleges, it also holds a macabre past. Join Heritage Ambassador Sherri La Torre and The History Center in Tompkins County for a haunted exploration of murder, jealousy, insanity, and perhaps some ghostly visits along the way. This program is for ages 16 to 116. The tour will take about 1 hour of walking in Downtown Ithaca. Group size is limited, and distancing and mask wearing among participants will be strictly enforced. Tickets are $15 per person.

Goblin Market- October 21 @ 5:00 pm - 8:00 pm; Buffalo Street Books 215 N. Cayuga St., Ithaca

Get spooky and crafty with us at the Goblin Market, our first “Makers” Market” of the fall/early winter! Held in Buffalo Street Books’ Reading Room, the event will showcase food, jewelry, art & more created by some of our favorite local artists & makers. Participants include Wobot and Friends, Water of Whimsy, Raven Magill, Rathaus Press, Rachel Radish Designs, Oracle Chocolates, Mar’s Studio, Kendra Saldana Ceramics, Kellie’s Artworks, Illuminated Press, Dani Mercury, Coaltown Vintage, Cat Crochets, and Brigh the Witch.

Halloween in Downtown Ithaca- October 28 @ 4:00 pm - 6:00 pm; Downtown Ithaca

Calling all costumed characters, ghouls, goblins, witches, superheroes, etc. Visit Downtown Ithaca from 4 to 6 p.m. on Friday, Oct. 28, to enjoy our family-friendly Halloween activities. The Downtown Ithaca Alliance (DIA) is teaming up with the Greater Ithaca Activities Center (GIAC) to host this fun annual tradition on and around the Ithaca Commons. This spooktacular event will include a community costume parade and store-to-store trick-or-treating at participating locations. (participating locations TBD). The community parade will start at GIAC, 301 W. Court St., Ithaca, at 3:30 p.m and then participants will march to The Commons on E. State Street, Ithaca.
